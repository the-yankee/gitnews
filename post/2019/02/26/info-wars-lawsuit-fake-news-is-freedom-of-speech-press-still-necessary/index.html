Info Wars Lawsuit - Fake News - Is Freedom of Speech & Press Still Necessary?

February 26, 2019 Congress

Alex Jones, spokesman, and founder of the streaming network, Info Wars, has been filed suit against by families of the Sandy Hook Massacre survivors.

In February, it was released that families had filed suit against Jones in a defamation trial, accusing him of misinformation on behalf of the deaths of their children.

Jones claimed, on his podcast, that the victims and shooter in the massacre were indeed actors.

The families declared this statement “a conspiracy theory”, disapproving of the statement and the story, as a whole.

Eventually, the case was put on hold, requiring Jones to turn in all tax returns, personal info, company info, and more. The case will be revisited, determining whether Jones abused his freedom of speech or not.

His “freedom of speech”, what is that?

Freedom of speech is a civil liberty, granted by the First Amendment of The U.S. Constitution. It grants all citizens the right to express their thought and beliefs, given that they don’t incite violence or danger.

James Madison, the author of the Federalist papers & The U.S. Constitution, believed in the importance of freedom of speech as a preservation of the liberty of ideas and thoughts among the common people. This is exactly as Madison intended it to be interpreted, leaving a mark of liberties on the American Constitution.

But, that was in 1791. We now live in the 21st century, a time of new ideas, new dangers, and a new way of living. Is the threat of speech too dangerous to be left without regulation?

In Jones’ case, his exclamation which targets the victims as villains of the violent act does not incite or encourage violence against the victims. In Jones’ case, he did, however, release private information about individuals, not being granted the authority to release that information. While exercising his freedom of speech, he does cross the line when he abuses his authority to hurt another citizen.

However, Jones, with his projection of conspiracy theories, against the victims, is not unlawful.
