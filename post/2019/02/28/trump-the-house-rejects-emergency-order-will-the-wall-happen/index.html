Trump - The House Rejects Emergency Order - Will the Wall Happen?

February 28, 2019 Trump's America

On February 26, President Donald Trump’s emergency executive order to bypass Congress and change the course of funding to a border wall was blocked, in a vote of 245 to 182.

Earlier this year, President Trump has rejected the proper funding for a border wall, calling for a government shutdown. Ever since, Trump’s goal has been to ensure a future of a southern border with a physical barrier. Later, President Trump declared a national emergency, had the conflict not been resolved by February 15th, allowing production of a border wall without support in the legislative body.

Now, Trump’s “national emergency” has been dismissed, not gaining any support or legislation for a full production of a national border wall.

It seems like Trump’s border wall has just been shut down time after time, making a chance of a possible border wall diminish increasingly. However, there is still a chance of a southern border wall.

First, let’s look at the constitutional justifications and warrantings of a possible southern border wall.

Speaker of the House, Nancy Pelosi, during the vote, exclaimed, “Is your oath of to Donald Trump or is it to the Constitution of the United States?”. This exclamation gives us a lens over Donald Trump’s border wall; begging the question, is a border wall constitutional?

The question of a border wall is justified by the 13th & 14th Amendments of the American Bill of Rights, stating, that the well-being of citizens of the United States is to be always put before those of Non-U.S. citizens.

The idea and practice of sanctuary cities and states are therefore unconstitutional, providing legal and constitutional justification for a border wall.

As well, The National Emergencies Act of 1986, passed by Congress, allows the president to declare the country in a state of national emergency, given that ⅔ of the legislative body approves of this.

While there is much debate on the issue of a southern border wall, there is no question that right now, the border wall is entirely justified on constitutional grounds and legal grounds. The question of the border wall being justified on moral grounds is to be determined by us, the people.
