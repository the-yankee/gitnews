Congress - Passes Bill to Require Extended Gun Background Checks

February 28, 2019 Congress

On February 28th, Congress passed a bill, requiring all gun manufacturers to take extended background checks with all gun sales. Rep. Mike Thomas, in district CA, and Pete King, New York Representative, proposed the bill, seeking reform with illegal and unlicensed firearms.

This act clamps down on drug dealers, restricting all from transferring illegal or unlicensed firearms to third parties until a background check has been completed.

Nowadays, current laws allow the sale to still commence, given that the provided dealer’s discretion is approved, even if the background check is not completed in a 3 days time span.

However, executive authority has not been a huge fan of this bill, stating “The extensive regulation required by H.R. 8 is diminishing of the Second Amendment and it human right to own a firearm.”. The White House said no veto would be involved, given that it passes through the House and Senate.

Despite executive disapproval, the bill was still passed, now transferring a national requirement to the NRA. The new laws will soon be enforced by all firearm dealers and manufacturers.

But, are The White House’s concerns about the constitutional justifications relevant?

Even if they are, Article V: Section 1: Clause 2 restricts any authority of any executive veto when Congress receives ⅔ of the vote, making the White House’s concerns invalid.

But, looking at their concerns, they have a little bit of ground. The Second Amendment of the Constitution gives the human right, to all, to own a licensed firearm. If this bill declines the civil liberty to certain U.S. individuals, it’s therefore somewhat unconstitutional, given that it enforces this upon non-criminals.
