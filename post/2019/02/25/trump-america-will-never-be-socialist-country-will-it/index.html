Trump - America Will Never Be Socialist Country -- Will it?

February 25, 2019 Presidents

President Donald Trump, ever since he has taken office, has taken an anti-socialist stance, while promoting capitalist ideals.

Will and should America ever be a socialist country?

The discussion, regarding socialism vs capitalism, has gained many spectators, as they are seen as the only two futures of the economic & political structure of our country. Should socialism, a system which benefits the society as a whole replace capitalism, a system which benefits the individuals within society?

However, America is already pretty much “socialist”. Wait, what?

First, let’s define socialism. By Webster’s definition, oversimplified, Socialism is a political or economic system or theory of social organization in which the community controls the means of production. By this definition, it is safe to say that programs in which the government controls funds for the general public are therefore socialist.

By this definition, ever since, the 1950s, with Dwight D. Eisenhower enacting many public services acts, allowing governmental funding for new public goods: such as welfare, public school systems, and more. Even before Eisenhower, the United States adopted many socialist aspects, like national defense funding.

However, there has never been a “socialist” political system. Sure, there have been “socialist” countries, such as Venezuela or Cuba, which have taken on the identity of socialism. However, these countries eventually resort to totalitarianism, to maintain order.

Will socialism always end in a totalitarian rule? If so, that is a huge danger to our country and people. In America, it might not.

Regardless, will the United States adopt a stance with more socialist ideologies? Will it keep its traditional capitalist beliefs that built the country?

It seems that politicians such as Bernie Sanders have been gaining much support from the general public, endorsing socialists ideals: such as universal healthcare, welfare, and more. These are generally socialist, as it grants control to the government over public goods & funds.

So, America is already socialist, let’s just adopt a fully socialist society, right?

However, this is potentially a dangerously unlimited amount of power which would be granted to the federal government.

Should the country adopt a system such as this? It depends on your views; are you willing to rely on the government to provide for you? Do you want to grant the government this much power?

This is the large risk for the true goal and reward of equality.
