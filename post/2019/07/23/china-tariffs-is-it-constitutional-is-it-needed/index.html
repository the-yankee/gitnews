China & Tariffs - Is it Constitutional - Is it Needed?

July 23, 2019 Trump

Much hysteria has appeared over the past few days, in response to President Donald Trump’s recent comments on being somewhat supportive of tariffs on Chinese trade.

So what’s all this? Trump? Tariffs? These have pretty much become swear words depending on who you’re talking to. First, let’s break this down in a non-controversial way.

Ok, so- what’s a tariff? A tariff, according to Meriam Webster (link down below), is: (noun) A schedule of duties imposed by a government on imported or in some countries exported goods. In simplified terms, it’s a government gaining or imposing a tax on chosen imported or exported products from or to their market. It’s kind of like when you buy something on eBay and the seller makes you pay for the shipping if that product is pretty cheap. President Trump, in simplified terms, wants to have the Chinese pay for their shipping.

However horrible everyone thinks of them nowadays, taxes are not the disgusting things that we think them to be. Without taxes, that given eBay seller can’t sell you anything because he’s gone out of business as he’s making no profits. The government requires taxes, without it, it cannot function- the debate of taxes comes to the extent to which they’re applied.

“Well I hate it when the eBay seller makes me pay for shipping, so why are we doing that to a whole country?” This is where the controversy comes into President Trump’s support of Chinese tariffs.

Well, it is quite annoying when you have to pay for your shipping- only, China has been making us pay for it, for years now! President Trump is simply attempting to spread the wealth between the States and China.

But that’s not it. More controversy comes with it with this given piece of history. Republicans across the board have been anti-taxation, supporting actions like the Tea Party and more. Why now are Trump and the Republicans supporting tariffs?

It’s probably safe to say that President Trump is not a traditional Republican and not a traditional president for that matter. However, the tariffs could impact the American economy in the same way that Herbert Hoover’s taxes leading us to the Great Depression or Rutherford B. Hayes’ Sherman plan didn’t work. It is a risk.

However long this topic has been around, it is far from over! Stay tuned and we will keep you updated!
